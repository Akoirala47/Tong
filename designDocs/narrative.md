# Tong: Project Narrative

## Core Vision

Tong is the world's first competitive language-learning ecosystem. It fuses three distinct interaction modes — a structured solo grind (PvE), asynchronous turn-based voice duels, and a live ranked voice arena (PvP) — into a single, cohesion-driven platform governed by a global ELO ladder.

Language fluency is not acquired by recognition alone. It is forged under pressure, in real-time, when the cost of hesitation is felt. Tong manufactures that pressure deliberately and repeatedly. Monetization gates **analysis depth**, not the ability to speak — free players get daily lite coaching; Plus removes ads and adds capped coaching; Pro unlocks unlimited Qwen-powered full analysis; Elite upgrades to Whisper turbo and phoneme-level feedback.

---

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Competitive gamers aged 18–30 who consume language-learning content but abandon passive apps due to lack of stakes or feedback loops they recognize from games.

**Secondary:** Language learners with an intermediate foundation (A2–B1) who have plateaued because they cannot find native or near-native speakers to practice with on demand.

**Tertiary:** Immigrants, expats, and professionals who need conversational fluency quickly and respond to outcome-based learning metrics over abstract progress percentages.

---

## Primary Problem

Every major language-learning app (Duolingo, Babbel, Pimsleur) teaches passive recognition. They reward streaks for completing low-stakes drills. They never force the learner to *produce* language under time pressure, in front of an opponent, with a rank on the line.

The gap they leave: **no app creates the productive discomfort of actually being tested by another human.** And the apps that do facilitate conversation (iTalki, Tandem) have no competitive structure, no matchmaking, no stakes, and no automated feedback loop.

Tong closes this gap entirely.

---

## The North Star

> **"Force players to speak before they feel ready."**

Every feature, mechanic, and data pipeline in Tong exists to serve a single guiding intuition: deliberate productive discomfort accelerates fluency faster than any passive drill. The system wins when a player stops mentally translating and starts producing language instinctively — because they have been repeatedly forced to do so against an opponent of their exact skill level.

The grind builds the vocabulary. The async duels build the reflex. The ranked arena tests whether the reflex holds under pressure.

---

## Strategic Differentiators

| Dimension | Traditional Apps | Tong |
|---|---|---|
| Feedback Loop | Passive recognition | Active production under pressure |
| Stakes | Streak maintenance | ELO rank, competitive ladder |
| Social Layer | Leaderboards | Live 1v1/squad voice battles |
| Feedback Depth | Correct/incorrect | Phoneme-level, WPM, formant analysis |
| Content Freshness | Curated static decks | Live-crawled slang, JIT-generated prompts |
| Engagement Model | Habit loops | Esport-style ranked seasons |

---

## Success Metric (North Star Metric)

**Weekly Active Speakers (WAS):** The number of distinct users who complete at least one live ranked voice battle or async audio duel per week. This metric is chosen over DAU because it specifically measures whether users are *speaking*, not just grinding flashcards. A user who only does solo lessons has not yet fulfilled Tong's core promise.
